# Let's Step It Up A Little - Breakdown Part 3

OK, this one looks longer, but you'll recognise most of it:

- **Customer**
> Ó, agus fianán freisin, le do thoil.
> Oh, ah-gus FEE-uh-nawn fuh-RESH-in, leh duh hull.
> Oh, and a cookie as well, please.

- **Barista**
> Ceart go leor. Sin ocht euro fiche ansin.#
> Kyart guh lur. Shin ukht YUR-oh FIH-heh an-shin.
> OK. That’s eight euro twenty then.

- **Barista**
> Cárta nó airgead tirim?
> KAR-tuh noh AR-gid TIR-im?
> Card or cash?

- **Customer**
> Cárta.
> KAR-tuh.
> Card.

- **Barista**
> Go raibh maith agat. Beidh siad réidh i gceann cúpla nóiméad.
> Guh rev mah ah-gut. Bay shid ray i gyown KOO-pluh NOH-mid.
> Thank you. They’ll be ready in a couple of minutes.

`Ó` is simply the Irish spelling of the English “oh”, which is used exactly the same way.

`Fianán freisin` means "a cookie as well."   `Freisin` is a very useful word meaning "also", "too" or "as well."

You might also hear:
- `Mise freisin.` – "Me too."
- `Caife freisin.` – "A coffee as well."

When the order changes, the barista updates the price:

`Sin ocht euro fiche ansin.`
You’ve already seen this structure, its just with a different number.

And finally:

`Beidh siad réidh i gceann cúpla nóiméad`  which means "They’ll be ready in a couple of minutes."

You don’t need to take this apart yet — just recognise it as a very common café phrase.

You’ll hear similar variations often:
- `Beidh sé réidh i gceann nóiméid.` – It’ll be ready in a minute.
- `Beidh siad réidh go luath.` – They’ll be ready soon.

This kind of timing language will come up regularly.
