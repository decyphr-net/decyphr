# Let's Step It Up A Little - Breakdown Part 2

The next few parts of the conversation also have a couple of very useful everyday phrases. Let's take a look:

- **Barista**
> Ceart go leor. Rud ar bith eile?
> Kyart guh lur. Rud er bih EL-eh?
> OK. Anything else?

- **Customer**
> Sin uile.
> Shin IL-eh.
> That’s everything.

- **Barista**
> Sin sé euro fiche.
> Shin shay YUR-oh FIH-heh.
> That’s six euro twenty.

`Rud ar bith eile` means "anything else?"

You’ll hear `ar bith` a lot in Irish. It adds that familiar “at all” feeling we often use in Irish English.

For example:
- `Rud ar bith eile?` – Anything else?
- `Fadhb ar bith?` – Any problem?
- `Ceist ar bith?` – Any question?

It’s a very common pattern.

`Sin uile` is a short, natural way of saying “that’s everything” or “that’s all.”

You might also hear:
- `Sin é.` – That’s it.
- `Sin gach rud.` – That’s everything.

And finally:
`Sin sé euro fiche.`

You’ve already seen this structure with prices.
It’s the same pattern, just with a different number.
