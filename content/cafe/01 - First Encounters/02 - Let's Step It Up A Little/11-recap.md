# Recap

Take a second to think about how this conversation started compared to where it ended.

At the beginning, you were simply placing an order. Now, you’ve handled follow-up questions about milk, chosen different sizes for different drinks, decided whether you’re staying or taking it away, added something after the price was given, adjusted to a new total, paid, waited, and collected your drinks.

That’s a full café interaction with multiple decision points built in.

Notice something important: nothing dramatic happened. The conversation didn’t become chaotic. Each extra question was short. Each answer was manageable. The structure stayed steady even as more pieces were added.

That’s how real conversations work.

They expand slightly, they branch a little, and then they settle again.

At this point, you can:
- Order more than one item
- Specify milk
- Choose sizes
- Decide for here or takeaway
- Add something late
- Handle price changes
- Close the interaction naturally

You’re not just repeating lines anymore. You’re responding to what’s happening in front of you.

Before we move on to taking this outside into the real world, we’re going to reinforce what you’ve just built. First, we’ll expand your vocabulary slightly so you have more options. Then you’ll put everything together in one final bonus order.

After that, it’s time to use it.

You’ve already done the hard part - now we strengthen it.
