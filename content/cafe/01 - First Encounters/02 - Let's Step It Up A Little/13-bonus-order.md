# Bonus Order - Layer It All Together


Right — time to build the full version.

No prompts. No subtitles. No looking back.

Walk into the coffee shop and place a layered order entirely in Irish.

Build the whole interaction:

Greeting → Multiple drinks → Milk choice → Size choice → For here or takeaway → Late add-on → Price update → Payment → Collection → Goodbye.

---

## Your Scenario

Order:
- Two drinks (different drinks)
- Two different sizes
- A milk alternative in one of them
- One food item added after the first price is given

You decide:
- Are you staying or taking it away?
- Are both drinks the same size or different?
- Does the barista ask, or do you volunteer the information?


---

## Do It Twice

1️⃣ First version:
The barista asks all clarification questions.

2️⃣ Second version:
You volunteer most of the information up front.

---

## If That Felt Comfortable, Level It Up

- Change one drink after you’ve ordered it.
- Add something small at the very end.
- Swap the price for a different number.
- Respond naturally to “Rud ar bith eile?” without pausing.

Keep it steady. Don’t rush it.

The goal isn’t speed. It’s control.

If you can build this conversation without stopping to translate in your head, you’re no longer repeating phrases — you’re operating within the structure.

And that’s the difference.
