# Let's Step It Up A Little - Breakdown Part 4

OK, we've reached the last few pieces of this conversation which take place after the drinks are ready:

- **Barista**
> Seo duit! Is é seo an latte.
> Sho gwich! Iss ay sho un LAH-teh.
> Here you are! This is the latte.

- **Customer**
> Go raibh míle maith agat.
> Guh rev MEE-leh mah ah-gut.
> Thanks a million.

- **Barista**
> Fadhb ar bith.
> Fowb er bih.
> No problem.

When the drinks are ready, the barista hands them over.

`Seo duit` - "Here you are."
You’ve already seen this one.

`Is é seo an latte` is a clear way of identifying something - "This is the latte."

You might also hear:
- `Agus is é seo an tae glas.` – "And this is the green tea."
- `Is é seo do chaife.` – "This is your coffee."

You don’t need to break this structure apart, just recognise it as a useful way of pointing something out clearly.

Next, the customer says:

`Go raibh míle maith agat.`

You’ve already seen `Go raibh maith agat.`  Adding `míle` (a thousand) turns it into "Thanks a thousand", or more naturally, "Thanks a million."

And finally:
`Fadhb ar bith.`

You’ve seen `ar bith` before. It adds that familiar "at all" feeling, so this phrase means “no problem at all.”

You might also hear:
- `Ná habair é.` – "Don’t mention it."
- `Tá fáilte romhat.` – "You’re welcome." (Can also be abbreviated to `Fáilte`)

And that brings the conversation to a natural close.
