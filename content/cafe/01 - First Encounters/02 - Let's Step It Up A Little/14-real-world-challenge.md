# Real World Challenge

This time, we’re raising it slightly.

Your challenge:

Use more than one line.

Place a simple order in Irish — and stay in it long enough to answer one follow-up question.

That’s it.

It could be:
- Choosing a size.
- Saying whether it’s for here or takeaway.
- Responding to “Rud ar bith eile?”
- Adding something small at the end.

You don’t need the full layered conversation.

You don’t need to get every detail perfect.

If they switch to English — fine.

If they repeat the question — fine.

If you answer partly in Irish and partly in English — also fine.

The goal is not fluency.

The goal is staying in the interaction just a little longer than last time.

In Unit 1, you proved you could say something.

In Unit 2, you prove you can handle a response.

Next time you’re at the counter, don’t overthink it.

Start in Irish.

Stay in it for one extra exchange.

That’s progress.

Now go use it.
