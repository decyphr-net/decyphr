# Small Or Large

OK, let's take a look at another way that the conversation may change at this same point in the conversation:

- **Barista**
> Dia duit! Cad is féidir liom a fháil duit?
> DEE-ah gwich! Cod iss FEH-dir lum uh ayl gwich?
> Hello! What can I get for you?

- **Customer**
> Dia is Muire duit. Caifé latte agus tae glas, le do thoil.
> DEE-ah iss MWIR-eh gwich. Kaff-AY LAH-teh ah-gus teh gloss, leh duh hull.
> Hi, a latte and a green tea, please.

- **Barista**
> Cén bainne atá uait sa latte?
> Kayn BAN-yuh ah-TAW oo-it suh LAH-teh?
> Which milk would you like in the latte?

- **Customer**
> Bainne coirce, le do thoil.
> BAN-yuh KUR-keh, leh duh hull.
> Oat milk, please.

- **Barista**
> Beag nó mór?
> Byug noh moar?
> Small or large?

There are a few different ways that this could be answered, depending on the number of drinks. For example, if you want both to be small, you could say:

- **Customer:**
> Beag, le do thoil.
> Byug, leh duh hull.
> Small, please.

or

> Mór, le do thoil.
> Moar, leh duh hull.
> Large, please.

Now, if you want one small and one large, you just need to be slightly more specific.

For example:

- **Customer**
> Latte mór agus tae beag, le do thoil.
> LAH-teh moar ah-gus teh byug, leh duh hull.
> A large latte and a small tea, please.

Or reversing it:

> Latte beag agus tae mór, le do thoil.
> LAH-teh byug ah-gus teh moar, leh duh hull.
> A small latte and a large tea, please.

You’ll notice we’re simply attaching the size to the drink. Nothing complicated, just the drink followed by the size.
