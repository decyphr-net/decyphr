# Let's Step It Up A Little - Breakdown Part 1

- **Barista**
> Dia duit! Cad is féidir liom a fháil duit?
> DEE-ah gwich! Cod iss FEH-dir lum uh ayl gwich?
> (Hello! What can I get for you?)

- **Customer**
> Dia is Muire duit. Caifé latte agus tae glas, le do thoil.
> > DEE-ah iss MWIR-eh gwich. Kaff-AY LAH-teh ah-gus teh gloss, leh duh hull.
> (Hi, a latte and a green tea, please.)

OK, here we see our greetings again. The difference is here is that the customer is ordering a latte (`caifé latte`) and a green tea (`tae glas`).

So, let's take a look at the next section, most of which you'll already be familiar with, again:
- **Barista**
> Cén bainne atá uait sa latte?
> > Kayn BAN-yuh ah-TAW oo-it suh LAH-teh?
> Which milk would you like in the latte?

- **Customer**
> Bainne coirce, le do thoil.
> BAN-yuh KUR-keh, leh duh hull.
> Oat milk, please.

Here's a very useful structure:
`Cén X atá uait?`

This is a very common way of asking “which X would you like?”

It’s a pattern you’ll be able to reuse in many situations:
- `Cén tae atá uait?` - "Which tea would you like?"
- `Cén méid atá uait?` - "Which size would you like?"
- `Cén ceapaire atá uait?` - "Which sandwich would you like?"
- `Cén bord atá uait?` - "Which table would you like?"

The next chunk then is `sa latte`. This just means "in the latte".

And the response is simple:
`Bainne coirce, le do thoil.`
